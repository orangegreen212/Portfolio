Prophet Method

Leveraging Facebook's Time Series Forecasting Library

Method Overview

Prophet is a forecasting library developed by Facebook specifically designed for time series data with strong seasonal effects, holidays, and missing data. It is particularly effective for business time series that often have daily, weekly, and yearly seasonality, plus irregular events. Prophet works by decomposing the time series into trend, seasonality, and holiday effects.

Data Used

For this demonstration of the Prophet method, I used the dataset on monthly online sales. The raw data covers the period from 2014 to 2017. For monthly analysis, the data was aggregated by month.

Forecasting Results & Analysis

The Prophet model was trained on the historical data. Below are plots visualizing the original data, the model's fit, and the forecasts on the test set and for future periods.

Raw Daily Sales Data:

This plot shows the raw daily sales data used in the project. It highlights the variability and potential seasonal patterns at the daily level.

Monthly Sales Forecast (Full Period):

This graph displays the monthly aggregated data, the model's fit on the training data, and the forecast on the test set and a short period into the future. It provides an overview of how Prophet captures the overall trend and seasonality.

Zoomed Comparison: Actual vs. Forecast on Test Set:

Focusing on the test period, this plot allows for a closer examination of Prophet's predictive performance on unseen data. It shows how closely the forecast aligns with the actual values in the last few months.

Forecast Components: Trend, Weekly, Yearly:

Prophet automatically provides a breakdown of the forecast into its constituent components: the long-term trend, weekly seasonality, and yearly seasonality. This helps understand the underlying patterns that influence the time series.
